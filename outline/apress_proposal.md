## Long Description (300–350 words — back cover copy)

Most development teams have tried using AI to generate code. The results are familiar: syntactically correct, superficially plausible, and frequently wrong in ways that take hours to diagnose. The model confidently generates structures that don't reflect the domain, schemas that miss the business rules, and APIs that look reasonable until a domain expert asks a single question and the whole thing unravels.

The missing ingredient isn't a better model or a cleverer prompt. It's a better specification. And that's where Domain-Driven Design comes in.

Beyond the Model shows how artificial intelligence enriches every step of the Synergetic Blueprint — a structured process that takes a software team from business intent all the way to running, tested, documented software. The book doesn't treat AI as a shortcut that replaces rigorous design thinking. It treats AI as a collaborator that makes rigorous design thinking faster, more consistent, and more productive.

The approach is empirically grounded. The running example — CookWithUs, a recipe-sharing platform — is built across three prototype iterations in published research, demonstrating that richer domain artifacts produce measurably better AI output at every step. Readers see exactly what AI gets right, what it gets wrong, and why the gaps are more valuable than the hits.

Seventeen chapters cover the complete design and delivery lifecycle: from AI-assisted North Star definition and Wardley Mapping through Domain Storytelling, EventStorming, Context Mapping, and Domain Modeling, to EXACT Coding — a battle-tested workflow for AI-collaborative, test-driven implementation that prevents vibe-coding from undermining the domain model.

Chapter 15, the keystone chapter on EXACT Coding, is co-authored by Ferdinand Ade and Marco Emrich of codecentric AG, bringing direct workshop experience to the most practically demanding section of the book.

By the end of the book, readers have a complete, proven workflow for AI-assisted domain-driven software development — one that keeps human expertise at the center while letting AI do what it does best.

## USP of the Book (4–5 bullet points)

- **Empirically grounded, not speculative.** The book is built on a published three-iteration prototyping pipeline demonstrating that richer domain artifacts produce measurably better AI output. Readers see real artifacts, real AI output, real gaps — not theoretical claims.
- **A complete process, not a collection of tips.** The Synergetic Blueprint covers the full lifecycle from business ideation to deployed, documented software. This is the only book that shows AI augmentation at every step of that process.
- **The EXACT Coding keystone.** Chapter 15, co-authored by Ferdinand Ade and Marco Emrich of codecentric AG, provides a battle-tested answer to the question every team asks: "What does AI-assisted DDD actually look like at the keyboard?"
- **One running example, fully traceable.** CookWithUs threads through all 17 chapters — every artifact, every AI interaction, every design decision is connected. Readers see how upstream modeling investments pay off in downstream implementation.
- **Durable across model generations.** The book teaches principles and artifact-based prompting strategies that work regardless of which LLM the reader is using. It will not be obsolete in six months.

**How Beyond the Model differs from these publications:**

The Evans and Vernon books are foundational DDD references but contain no treatment of AI augmentation and predate current LLM capabilities by more than a decade. They remain essential references; this book positions itself as the AI-era companion, not a replacement.

The Hofer/Schwentner Domain Storytelling book covers one collaborative modeling technique in depth but does not address AI augmentation or the broader Blueprint process. The Ford/Richards architecture book addresses distributed systems trade-offs but not DDD practice or AI augmentation. The Yanev book covers ChatGPT API integration for application building but has no connection to domain modeling, collaborative design practice, or the structured process that makes AI output reliable. None of these books addresses the gap this proposal fills.

Mastering Domain-Driven Design (Junker, BPB, 2025) establishes the Synergetic Blueprint that this book augments with AI. It is a companion, not a competitor — readers of that book are the primary audience for this one.

No existing publication covers AI augmentation of the full DDD lifecycle from ideation to implementation with an empirically grounded, process-driven approach.
